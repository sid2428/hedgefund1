ITEM 1A. RISK FACTORS

Customer Concentration. A significant portion of our revenue is derived from a small number
of large customers. In fiscal 2024, two customers each accounted for more than 10% of
total revenue. Our largest customer, Apple Inc., represented approximately 22% of fiscal
2024 revenue, and our second-largest customer represented approximately 14%. The loss
of, or a significant reduction in orders from, any of these customers could have a material
adverse effect on our business.

Supply Chain Concentration. We rely on Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Company
("TSMC") as our primary foundry partner for the production of substantially all of our
advanced GPUs and AI accelerators. We do not have a long-term volume guarantee
agreement with TSMC, and any disruption to TSMC's operations or capacity could
materially affect our ability to deliver products to customers. We are exploring
diversification of foundry partners but expect TSMC to remain our largest supplier through
fiscal 2025.

Geographic Exposure. Approximately 30% of our fiscal 2024 revenue was generated from
customers in China. Continued export controls and trade restrictions imposed by the U.S.
government on advanced semiconductors sold into China could materially reduce demand.

ITEM 7. MANAGEMENT'S DISCUSSION AND ANALYSIS OF FINANCIAL CONDITION AND
RESULTS OF OPERATIONS

Outlook. While demand for AI-accelerated computing remains robust, we are observing
elongated sales cycles in certain end markets and we expect demand to remain volatile
through the first quarter as macro conditions evolve. We are taking a more cautious view
of the remainder of fiscal 2025 than we communicated last quarter. Capital expenditures
for fiscal 2025 are expected to be in the range of $4.0 billion to $4.5 billion, an increase
from the $3.4 billion incurred in fiscal 2024 to support data center capacity expansion.
